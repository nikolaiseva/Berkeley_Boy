“The Golden Girl Didn’t Need to Rewrite Her Story—Just a Berkeley Boy to Bring Peace Between the Pages”

A quiet love story behind Sabrina Carpenter’s surprise album Berkeley Boy

It seems love might be blooming far from the red carpets and flashing cameras. Pop star Sabrina Carpenter, known for her sparkling stage presence and heart-spun lyrics, has just released a surprise single titled “Berkeley Boy”—and fans believe it tells the story of a quiet, unexpected love that found her when she wasn’t looking.

Following her quiet breakup with actor Barry Keoghan last year, many wondered if—and when—she’d open her heart again. The world had watched them share the occasional spotlight, but as the cameras faded, so did the connection. What came next wasn’t a whirlwind—it was stillness, and then something real.

Now, sources close to the singer say the muse behind “Berkeley Boy” is a Canadian-born Master’s student in Engineering at UC Berkeley: someone charming, grounded, brilliant in the most unassuming way—and with a lifelong passion for ice hockey that keeps him both humble and free.

He’s not the type to be found in headlines. He’s the kind who stays late working on things that matter, builds with his hands, and speaks through gentle actions more than words. Friends describe him as thoughtful and sincere, with a quiet strength that makes people feel safe—someone who builds not just machines but meaning.

The two reportedly crossed paths at a Bay Area event earlier this year, where a simple conversation turned into something soft and magnetic. Her world, full of rhythm and light; his, full of structure and purpose—and somewhere in the middle, they found each other.
